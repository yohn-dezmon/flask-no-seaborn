Project Background

The Global Database of Events, Language, and Tone (GDELT) captures news articles from around the world and stores them in a database that can currently be accessed by the public using Google Cloud Services or Amazon Web Services. Please click here for an explanation of the details of the dataset and how it was used in this project.

I filtered the dataset for topics related to education in order to perform an exploratory analysis of how educational topics are covered within the US media. Some of the questions I was interested in answering include: which states have the greatest media coverage of educational topics, how are laws regarding education received by the public, what is the relative occurence of educational buzzwords in US media, amongst others.
