Keyword: Curriculum

This graph shows the amount of articles containing the keyword curriculum in their titles that were associated with a given state. California, Texas, Massachusetts and New York were the states associated with the greatest number of articles concerning curriculum; potentially suggesting controversy or perhaps reform. To find out why these states were high in events regarding curriculum, I querired the dataset for the articles with the highest number of mentions and the results can be found below.

{{ CA_html_table|safe }}

The graph on the left displays a histogram of the distribution of average tone of curriculum articles that were reported within California. The majority of the articles appear to have been slighly above 0, indicating a neutral or positive overall tone. On the right are the URLs which had the greatest number of mentions.

Looking at the URL paths from the table, we can glean that in general the articles covering curriculum in California are concerned with increasing computer science, reforming sexual education, incorporating LGBQT topics into curriculum, as well as expanding racial history/topics within both k-12 and university curriculums.

{{ TX_html_table|safe }}

Texas' Average Tone Distribution also hovers around 0, however the distribution does tilt a bit to the left of 0 indicating that there may have been more controversial content reported within Texas. Indeed, looking at the table, we see that one of the most mentioned articles is a feature reporting the elimination of Helen Keller and Hillary clinton from Texas schools' history curriculum.

{{ MA_html_table|safe }}

Masachusetts' distribution shifts back to the right, indicating an overall more positive tone. The most mentioned articles involving Massachusetts include articles discussing expanding curriculums to include improved foreign language courses, criticism of a college program focussed on social justice, reforms for math and science curriculum, and also a discussion of sexual education.
